"To be leaders among men"

"Lambda Phi Epsilon was founded on February 25, 1981 by a group of nineteen dedicated men led by principal founder Master Craig Ishigo. Thoe goal of the founders was to transcend the limitations faced by traditional Asian American interest organizations on campus. While these organizations were oftensplit along ines of national origins, the founders sought an establishment that drew its membership equally from the different segments of the Asian American community. Their vision was that the members would eventually become the leaders of their respective ethnic communities, and thus, bridge the gaps fragmenting the Asian American community through their affiliation with a common organization. Today, Lambda Phi Epsilon stands as the LARGEST and GREATEST Asian-interest fratnerity in the world."
